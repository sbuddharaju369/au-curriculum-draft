# Day 50 — Retrospective & Next-Step Reflection

> **Outcome of the day:** every student leaves with (a) written reflection, (b) a 1:1 with a facilitator, (c) a concrete next step toward Oxmiq or graduate work or industry.
> **Mindset:** the internship doesn't end at the door. Day 50 is the launchpad.

## Team retrospective (45 min, facilitated)

The team that did the capstone together sits with one facilitator. Format:

| Time | Topic |
|---|---|
| 0–10 | What worked well as a team (each person says one thing) |
| 10–20 | What was hard (each person says one thing — interpersonal OK, no blame) |
| 20–30 | What we'd do differently if we did this capstone again |
| 30–40 | What we learned about how we like to work — and what that means for our next role |
| 40–45 | Quick appreciations: each person thanks one other team member for one specific thing |

Facilitator takes notes (private to the team) and shares back a one-paragraph summary by email within a week.

---

## 1:1 with a facilitator (15 min each)

Each student gets a 15-min conversation with a facilitator. Agenda:

1. **5 min — career-direction calibration.** Based on the 10 weeks, what role do you think suits you? Facilitator pushes back gently if the read seems off.
2. **5 min — Oxmiq-specific pathways.** Open roles, internship-to-FTE conversion (if applicable), referral mechanics, what to put on a resume from this internship.
3. **5 min — outside pathways.** Graduate programs that align (with names), open-source projects to contribute to, companies hiring in this space, specific Oxmiq alumni you can email.

Facilitator leaves the student with **one concrete next action** ("apply to X by Y date" / "email Z by next Friday" / "ship one PR to the Capsule repo by end of month"). The action goes in writing.

## Oxmiq pathway notes (reference for facilitators)

These are the most common "next steps" the program has placed alumni into. Use as a starter menu in the 1:1.

| Pathway | Typical signal | What to do next |
|---|---|---|
| Oxmiq FTE — Inference engineer | Strong Capstone + comfortable with engine internals | Resume review with facilitator; intro to hiring manager |
| Oxmiq contractor — Capsule integrator | Strong Weeks 6–9 work; less interested in core eng | Facilitator intro; trial project |
| Oxmiq alumni / open source contributor | Wants to keep contributing while exploring | List of "good first issues" in Capsule repo |
| Graduate school — Systems / ML | Wants depth in fundamentals | Reading list + 2–3 program suggestions |
| Industry — other inference-platform companies | Wants similar role elsewhere | Companies hiring + referral asks |
| Industry — applied ML / product | Liked the prompt engineering & evaluation work more than the systems work | Different companies + targeting tips |
| Not sure yet | Honest "don't know" | Suggest 2 specific projects that would clarify direction |

The 1:1 isn't a placement guarantee; it's a calibration plus a concrete first move.

## Cohort-wide closing (30 min)

Facilitator:

1. Names the best work of the cohort — by team and by individual contribution. Specific, not generic.
2. Shares the 3 things this cohort taught the program (from anonymized retrospective notes).
3. Announces what changes for the next cohort.
4. Adds everyone to the alumni Slack / mailing list. Names one prior alum still in touch as proof the network is real.

## What "graduating" actually means here

You leave knowing:

1. How to write a prompt that an LLM can reliably act on, and how to measure when it stops working.
2. How an inference engine actually runs that prompt — every layer down to KV cache and quantization.
3. How to combine LLMs into agents that act in the world without breaking everything.
4. How to deploy and operate a real model on real hardware, with real cost and latency numbers, and recommend it with confidence.

That combination is rare. Now go use it.
