> **Source templates:** [Capstone Guide](../../../../planning/source-material/Capstone/Capstone-Guide.md) + Day-46–50 templates linked from the [week overview](../index.md) (source filenames retain upstream capstone-relative naming; they map to program Days 47–51).

## The deliverable

A team-defended **decision** about deploying an LLM on Capsule for a real use case, expressed as the claim sentence:

> **"For use case X, deploy model Y at config Z, because [evidence from benchmark] shows [metric] at [cost], with [quality tradeoff] that is [acceptable / not] because [reasoning]."**

## Grading rubric (Day 50 panel)

Per [Day-49 Assessment Rubric](../../../../planning/source-material/Capstone/Day-49-Assessment-Rubric.md), 8 dimensions equally weighted:

1. Use case clarity
2. Technical depth (Phase 1 vocabulary correct throughout)
3. Methodology rigor (sweep + controls + eval executed)
4. Evidence quality (table complete; cost math correct)
5. Recommendation strength (claim sentence defensible; tradeoffs honest)
6. Q&A handling (composed; admits limits)
7. Teamwork (visible contribution from each member)
8. Presentation polish

**Pass:** 6/8 dimensions at "meets bar" or above.
**Distinction:** 7/8, with at least one "exceeds bar".

Team grade applies to all members; instructor reserves right to adjust by ±1 grade level per individual based on observed Day-47–51 contribution.

## What this assignment is testing

Everything. Phase 1 (inference vocabulary), Phase 2 (prompt + agent thinking when defending choices), Phase 3 (Capsule fluency to actually execute). The capstone is the only place all three are tested together under time pressure with a real artifact at the end.

## What this assignment is *not* testing

- **Building something novel** — pick a real, simple use case. Sophistication ≠ value.
- **Beating a state-of-the-art benchmark** — defending your numbers matters far more than the numbers themselves.
- **Trying every model** — one model, one sweep done well > five models done badly.
